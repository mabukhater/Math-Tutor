# Vetted-question review sample

Total vetted pulled: 1000. 3 random per curriculum+grade.


## Singapore Math — Grade 1

- **Compare and order numbers within 100**
  - Q: Which number comes between 56 and 58?
  - Options: A) 55 | B) 57 | C) 59 | D) 60
  - Answer: B) 57
  - Why: 57 is between 56 and 58 on the number line.
- **Multiplication as repeated addition (groups of)**
  - Q: There are 3 bags. Each bag has 4 sweets. How many sweets in all?
  - Options: A) 7 | B) 12 | C) 9 | D) 8
  - Answer: B) 12
  - Why: 4 + 4 + 4 = 12 sweets.
- **Number bonds within 10**
  - Q: The whole is 9 and one part is 0. What is the other part?
  - Options: A) 0 | B) 1 | C) 9 | D) 10
  - Answer: C) 9
  - Why: 9 - 0 = 9, so the other part is 9.

## Singapore Math — Grade 3

- **Find the area and perimeter of figures**
  - Q: A figure is made of 7 unit squares. What is its area?
  - Options: A) 7 cm² | B) 7 unit squares | C) 14 unit squares | D) 28 unit squares
  - Answer: B) 7 unit squares
  - Why: Each square is one unit, so 7 squares give an area of 7 unit squares.
- **Add and subtract like fractions**
  - Q: What is 4/9 - 1/9?
  - Options: A) 3/9 | B) 3/0 | C) 5/9 | D) 3/18
  - Answer: A) 3/9
  - Why: Subtract the numerators: 4 - 1 = 3, and keep the denominator 9, giving 3/9.
- **Equivalent fractions and comparing fractions**
  - Q: Which fraction is NOT equivalent to 1/2?
  - Options: A) 3/5 | B) 2/4 | C) 4/8 | D) 5/10
  - Answer: A) 3/5
  - Why: 3/5 is not half because 5 is not double 3, while the others all simplify to 1/2.

## Singapore Math — Grade 4

- **Add and subtract related fractions**
  - Q: What is 3/4 - 1/2?
  - Options: A) 1/4 | B) 2/2 | C) 2/4 | D) 1/2
  - Answer: A) 1/4
  - Why: Change 1/2 to 2/4, then 3/4 - 2/4 = 1/4.
- **Factors and multiples**
  - Q: Which number is a common multiple of 2 and 3?
  - Options: A) 4 | B) 6 | C) 9 | D) 8
  - Answer: B) 6
  - Why: 6 is in the times tables of both 2 (2 × 3) and 3 (3 × 2), so it is a common multiple.

## UK National Curriculum — Grade 2

- **Measure, compare, add and subtract lengths, mass and volume/capacity**
  - Q: What is 40 cm + 30 cm?
  - Options: A) 60 cm | B) 70 cm | C) 10 cm | D) 700 cm
  - Answer: B) 70 cm
  - Why: 40 + 30 = 70, so the answer is 70 cm.
- **Measure, compare, add and subtract lengths, mass and volume/capacity**
  - Q: Sam's pencil is 12 cm long and Mia's is 9 cm long. How much longer is Sam's pencil?
  - Options: A) 3 cm | B) 21 cm | C) 2 cm | D) 4 cm
  - Answer: A) 3 cm
  - Why: 12 cm − 9 cm = 3 cm, so Sam's pencil is 3 cm longer.
- **Measure, compare, add and subtract lengths, mass and volume/capacity**
  - Q: A bag of flour weighs 750 g. A bag of sugar weighs 250 g. What is their total mass?
  - Options: A) 1000 g | B) 500 g | C) 900 g | D) 1100 g
  - Answer: A) 1000 g
  - Why: 750 g + 250 g = 1000 g, which is the same as 1 kilogram.

## UK National Curriculum — Grade 3

- **Convert between units of measure (e.g. kilometres to metres)**
  - Q: A ribbon is 2 m and 50 cm long. What is its total length in centimetres?
  - Options: A) 250 cm | B) 205 cm | C) 2050 cm | D) 52 cm
  - Answer: A) 250 cm
  - Why: 2 m is 200 cm, and 200 + 50 = 250 centimetres.
- **Recognise and find equivalent fractions**
  - Q: Which fraction is equivalent to 2/4?
  - Options: A) 1/2 | B) 1/4 | C) 2/3 | D) 3/4
  - Answer: A) 1/2
  - Why: 2/4 simplifies to 1/2 by dividing both numbers by 2.
- **Multiply two- and three-digit numbers by a one-digit number**
  - Q: What is 48 × 7?
  - Options: A) 326 | B) 346 | C) 336 | D) 356
  - Answer: C) 336
  - Why: 7 × 40 = 280 and 7 × 8 = 56, so 280 + 56 = 336.

## US Common Core — Grade 3

- **Partition shapes into parts with equal areas**
  - Q: A circle is split into 2 equal pieces. Each piece is called what?
  - Options: A) A third | B) A half | C) A fourth | D) A whole
  - Answer: B) A half
  - Why: Two equal pieces means each piece is one half.

  - Answer: A) 3 × 4 = 12

## US Common Core — Grade 4

- **Generate and analyze number/shape patterns**
  - Q: What number comes next? 20, 18, 16, 14, __
  - Options: A) 13 | B) 12 | C) 11 | D) 10
  - Answer: B) 12
  - Why: The pattern subtracts 2 each time, so 14 - 2 = 12.
- **Multiplicative comparison word problems**

  - Options: A) 24 is 4 more than 6 | B) 24 is 4 times as many as 6 | C) 6 is 4 times as many as 24 | D) 24 is 6 more than 4
  - Answer: B) 24 is 4 times as many as 6
  - Why: 4 × 6 = 24 means 24 is 4 times as many as 6.
- **Place value: each place is 10x the place to its right**
  - Q: Which sentence is true?
  - Options: A) The hundreds place is 100 times the tens place | B) The tens place is 10 times the hundreds place | C) The hundreds place is 10 times the tens place | D) The ones place is 10 times the tens place
  - Answer: C) The hundreds place is 10 times the tens place
  - Why: Each place is 10 times the place to its right, so hundreds is 10 times tens.
